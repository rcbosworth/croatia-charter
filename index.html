§14. Tips & Warnings

  • Book immediately. July 26–August 2 is the single most expensive and most-booked week of the Croatian charter season. NCP Charter live search showed only 2 boats remaining for Jul 27–Aug 3. Availability is disappearing now.
  • Saturday–Saturday is almost universal. Your July 27 (Sunday) start eliminates most boats. Your strongest options are the Sun Odyssey 440 on 12knots (any day, min 3 nights), or asking FairWind/CharterSplit if they'll allow a Sunday start for the July 26–Aug 2 week.
  • Always book direct when possible. Aggregators add 10–20% commission. CharterSplit, FairWind, and ABA Vela are all direct operators with no middleman markup.
  • NCP Charter is Šibenik, not Split. Beautiful marina but ~80km and ~1 hour drive from Split. Factor in transfers.
  • Activity Yachting has strong July 26 availability. Their NauSYS widget has a date picker bug (calendar shows one month behind what it stores — click June 26 to set July 26). With correct dates searched: 22 sailing yachts available in Split region (€1,776–€7,163 in budget), plus the standout find — Excess 11 (2023) catamaran "First Sight" in Šibenik at €4,684/wk base, 4+2 cabins, brand new boat. Add €250/wk flotilla fee.
  • Mooring costs add up fast. Budget €50–€80/night at ACI marinas in peak season. For a 7-night trip stopping 5 nights in marinas, that's €250–€400 just in port fees. Anchoring in island bays (free) is preferred by experienced sailors.
  • Catamaran prices at peak. Split-based catamarans with 4+ cabins run €10,500–€21,000+ base in July. But two Šibenik-based options are dramatically cheaper: Activity Yachting Excess 11 (2023) "First Sight" at ACI Marina Skradin €4,684/wk (4+2 cabins, brand new — best cat deal overall, ~€6,650 all-in); and NCP Charter Lagoon 380 at €2,800/wk base from Mandalina Šibenik (~€5,326 all-in). Both ~35–40 min from Split.
  • FairWind and ABA Vela are the most consistently recommended direct operators in Split across sailing forums. Both communicate quickly and maintain their fleets well.
